RISE OF THE MACHINES

75 Years of Artificial Intelligence
Breakthrough Boom Era AI Winter
Foundations · The Field Is Born
1950

The Turing Test

Alan Turing publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," proposing the imitation game and asking whether machines can think.

Alan Turing
1956

Dartmouth Conference

John McCarthy coins the term "Artificial Intelligence" at a summer workshop that officially launches the field.

John McCarthy · Marvin Minsky
1958

The Perceptron

Frank Rosenblatt builds the first trainable neural network, hardware that learns from examples.

Frank Rosenblatt · Cornell
1966

ELIZA

MIT's first chatbot mimics a psychotherapist and convinces users it understands them, an early warning about how easily humans anthropomorphize machines.

Joseph Weizenbaum · MIT
❄ The Field Freezes
AI WINTER I
1974 – 1980

First AI Winter

The UK's Lighthill Report and DARPA funding cuts freeze the field. Symbolic AI overpromised; the era's computing power couldn't deliver.

Lighthill Report · DARPA
The Thaw · Expert Systems
1980 – 1987

Expert Systems Boom

Rule-based systems like XCON save DEC millions per year; Japan launches its Fifth Generation Computer project and corporate AI investment surges.

DEC · Japan MITI
❄ The Field Freezes Again
AI WINTER II
1987 – 1993

Second AI Winter

The LISP machine market collapses and expert systems prove brittle and costly to maintain. Investor confidence evaporates for years.

LISP Machines · Expert Systems Inc.
Quiet Progress · Machines Start Winning
1997

Deep Blue

IBM's chess machine defeats world champion Garry Kasparov, the first defeat of a reigning champion by a computer in match play.

IBM · Garry Kasparov
2006

Deep Learning Revival

Geoffrey Hinton's work on deep belief networks reignites neural network research after decades in the wilderness.

Geoffrey Hinton · U of Toronto
2011

IBM Watson

Watson defeats human champions Ken Jennings and Brad Rutter on Jeopardy!, proving machines can parse natural language at scale.

IBM
Ignition · Hardware Meets Theory
2012

AlexNet

A neural network trained on Nvidia GPUs crushes the ImageNet competition. Hardware finally catches up with 30-year-old theory, and deep learning takes off.

Krizhevsky · Sutskever · Hinton · Nvidia
2016

AlphaGo

Google DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats Go master Lee Sedol 4–1, mastering a game once thought decades beyond machines.

Google DeepMind · Lee Sedol
2017

The Transformer

Google researchers publish "Attention Is All You Need," the architecture behind every modern large language model.

Google Brain
2020

GPT-3

OpenAI's 175-billion-parameter model shows that scale itself produces new capabilities, trained on massive cloud compute.

OpenAI · Microsoft Azure
2022

ChatGPT

ChatGPT reaches an estimated 100 million users in two months, the fastest-growing consumer app in history. AI goes mainstream overnight.

OpenAI
2023 – 2026

The Frontier Race

OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic race to build ever-more-capable models on Nvidia-powered cloud infrastructure, transforming industries from software to medicine.

OpenAI · Google · Anthropic · Nvidia
